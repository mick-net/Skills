# Embedded Lenny-Style Solo-Founder Playbooks

Use these embedded playbooks before loading external Lenny skills. They capture the most relevant founder/indie-hacker slices and translate them away from big-company assumptions.

## Founder Sales

Use when there are no customers, few customers, or the founder is still building without buyer conversations.

Rules:
- Founder does sales personally.
- Sell before building more.
- Qualify for active pain, not politeness.
- A sale is not done until the buyer reaches value.

Actions:
- Write 20 specific warm/cold leads.
- Send 5-10 concise personalized messages.
- Offer a 15-minute diagnostic or manual setup.
- Ask for payment, pilot, or a concrete next step.

## Customer Interviews

Use when the product, buyer, or pain is unclear.

Ask:
- What are you doing today?
- What triggered the search for a better way?
- What is expensive, annoying, risky, or slow?
- What have you already tried?
- Who owns budget or approval?
- What would make this worth paying for?

Store notes under `company/customers/`.

## Problem Definition And Scoping

Use when the user has a feature idea but not a clear customer problem.

Cut until:
- One buyer.
- One painful job.
- One paid wedge.
- One proof action.
- One channel.

If the feature does not improve activation, conversion, retention, expansion, distribution, or support load, do not build it yet.

## Positioning

Use when the product is hard to explain.

Formula:

`For [specific buyer], [product] helps [painful job] by [mechanism], without [bad alternative].`

Landing page minimum:
- Buyer.
- Pain.
- Outcome.
- Proof.
- CTA.

## Content And Launch

Use when the user needs distribution.

Solo-founder content should come from real work:
- Demo a feature.
- Explain a customer pain.
- Publish a tutorial or free tool.
- Compare against a frustrating incumbent.
- Share a concrete result.
- Turn docs into SEO pages.

Launch minimum:
- Short demo.
- Specific buyer.
- Clear CTA.
- Proof or founder story.
- Follow-up posts.

## Onboarding And Retention

Use when traffic/signups exist but users do not activate or stay.

Check:
- Time to first value.
- First confusing step.
- Empty states.
- Required integrations/setup.
- Payment moment.
- Habit or repeat-use trigger.

Fix onboarding before adding more features.

## Pricing

Use when charging, packaging, or upgrading.

Rules:
- Charge earlier.
- Price against value and alternatives.
- One-time only when marginal cost/support is low.
- Subscription when the product has ongoing hosting, AI cost, sync, team value, support, or business value.
- Do not underprice B2B if support will be high.
