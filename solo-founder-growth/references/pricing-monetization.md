# Pricing And Monetization

Use this for pricing decisions, free/paid boundaries, lifetime vs subscription, and B2B expansion.

## Pricing Defaults

- Charge earlier than feels comfortable.
- Prefer simple tiers over custom complexity.
- Price against the value and alternative, not engineering time.
- Use payment as validation; waitlists and compliments are weaker signals.
- Raise prices as confidence, value, and trust increase.

## Free Boundary

Free is acceptable when it creates one of:
- Immediate product understanding.
- A shareable artifact.
- A qualified lead.
- A usage habit that naturally hits a paid limit.
- SEO or marketplace inventory.

Free is dangerous when:
- Users can get full value without paying.
- Support costs grow with free usage.
- AI/model/infrastructure costs are variable.
- Free users distort roadmap priorities.

## One-Time vs Subscription

Use one-time pricing when:
- Marginal cost is near zero.
- The product can run locally, statically, offline, or BYO-key.
- Support burden is low.
- Customers compare against templates, desktop apps, utilities, or owned software.
- A quick cash injection and lower purchase friction matter more than recurring revenue.

Use subscription when:
- You host infrastructure, sync, storage, AI usage, monitoring, or team features.
- The product delivers ongoing business value.
- Customer support and reliability costs recur.
- Teams, compliance, admin, or SLAs matter.
- Expansion revenue is possible.

Hybrid pattern:
- One-time personal/core license.
- Subscription add-ons for sync, cloud, teams, hosting, higher usage, compliance, or managed service.

## Usage-Based Tiers

Use when value scales with volume:
- Messages, seats, agents, documents, workflows, credits, exports, tracked visitors, jobs, listings.

Keep it understandable:
- Name the buyer type.
- Show the usage limit.
- Show the value difference.
- Make upgrade triggers obvious.

## B2B Expansion Timing

Add sales, Teams, or Enterprise when:
- Individuals ask to use it at work.
- Multiple people from the same domain sign up.
- Buyers ask for SSO, roles, audit logs, procurement, SOC 2, self-hosting, or custom deployment.
- You can charge enough to justify calls and support.
- The self-serve product already proves the core value.

Do not add B2B just because revenue sounds bigger. B2B adds trust, support, docs, security, and sales obligations.

## Pricing Experiments

Run one change at a time:
- Raise price for new customers.
- Add annual discount.
- Remove underpriced plan.
- Gate a power feature.
- Add usage expansion.
- Add a team plan.
- Test paid onboarding/setup.

Track:
- Visitor-to-signup.
- Signup-to-activation.
- Activation-to-paid.
- ARPA.
- Refunds.
- Churn.
- Support load.
- Gross margin.

## Pricing Output Template

- `Current buyer`: who pays and why.
- `Recommended model`: one-time, subscription, usage, hybrid, or service-first.
- `Free boundary`: what is free and where payment starts.
- `First price`: concrete number and rationale.
- `Upgrade path`: what creates higher ARPA.
- `Risk`: why this pricing may fail.
- `Experiment`: what to test next and what metric decides.
