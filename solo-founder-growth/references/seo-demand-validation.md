# SEO Demand Validation

Use this when the bottleneck is search demand, SEO, public-surface traffic, content gaps, free tools, pSEO, or backlink authority. Do not load it by default for every founder coaching request.

Core principle: search data is a demand signal and distribution map. It is not payment proof. Use it to find existing demand, choose the right public asset, and avoid building where no one is looking.

## When To Use

Use for:
- Prelaunch ideas that may be SEO-led.
- Launched products with no traffic.
- Public pages that do not match buyer/searcher intent.
- Deciding whether to build a free tool, comparison page, integration page, or content hub.
- `$1k+ MRR` products looking for repeatable organic acquisition.

Do not use for:
- Hidden backend work.
- Generic blog planning before buyer/proof is clear.
- Dashboard projects before the next MRR question is known.
- Broad pSEO before payment, a real data source, or clear long-tail demand.

## Search-Demand Gate

Before recommending SEO-led build work, answer:

- `Buyer/searcher`: who is searching, and are they the buyer or only a researcher?
- `Painful job`: what job, output, alternative, or problem are they searching for?
- `Search demand`: are there real searches across the main keyword and related terms?
- `Commercial value`: is there CPC, paid competition, purchase intent, or a believable paid offer?
- `Competition`: can a small site plausibly compete on authority, page quality, or niche specificity?
- `Intent`: what page type already ranks?
- `Ability to exceed`: can the founder create something meaningfully better this week?
- `Paid boundary`: where does the asset lead to payment, trial, setup, pilot, or email capture?

If search demand is weak and there is no reachable community/channel, route back to validation instead of building.

## Intent To Asset

Map the keyword to the asset searchers expect:

- `Transactional`: landing page, tool page, pricing, booking, checkout, or paid CTA.
- `Commercial`: comparison page, alternatives page, review/proof page, use-case page.
- `Informational`: guide, tutorial, example library, technical explainer, or content page.
- `Tool/generator`: working free tool, demo, calculator, generator, or template.
- `Integration`: "[product] for [platform]" page with setup proof and use case.
- `Navigational`: usually ignore for acquisition unless it exposes brand confusion.

Do not build the asset you prefer. Build the asset the searcher and current search results imply.

## Match And Exceed

For each target keyword or page:

1. Inspect the current top results.
2. Identify the dominant page type.
3. Note what is weak: outdated examples, shallow content, bad UX, missing tool, weak proof, slow page, no niche specificity, low authority.
4. Decide whether you can exceed it with current constraints.

If you cannot exceed the current results, do not spend founder time there yet. Pick a narrower long-tail keyword, another channel, or a manual validation action.

## Free Tool Guardrails

A free tool is worth building only when:

- It solves a searched job directly.
- It demonstrates the paid product's value.
- It can earn links or shares because it is useful on its own.
- It has a clear paid boundary, upsell, capture, or next action.
- It can be shipped quickly and maintained cheaply.

Do not build free tools that attract non-buyers, create support burden, or give away the full paid value.

## pSEO Guardrails

Use pSEO only when:

- There are many real long-tail intents.
- Each page can provide unique value, not just swapped keywords.
- You have a real data source, user-generated content, examples, templates, inventory, or integrations.
- Pages can be internally linked and grouped clearly.
- Indexing can start with a small high-quality batch.

Avoid:
- Thin AI-generated pages.
- Duplicate titles, descriptions, and body content.
- Keyword stuffing.
- Cannibalization: multiple pages targeting the same primary search job.
- Generating thousands of pages before knowing which pages deserve to rank.

AI can assist research, structure, drafts, and transformations. Do not publish generic AI slop without product evidence, human judgment, or unique value.

## Backlink Authority Guardrails

Backlinks amplify a strong page. They do not repair weak product, vague positioning, bad UX, or missing payment path.

Before backlink outreach, check:

- Is the page useful, fast, credible, and conversion-ready?
- Is there a specific reason another site would reference it?
- Does the target site already cover this job, competitor, category, or tool type?
- Would the link help a buyer, not only the founder?

Good backlink work:
- Competitor mention replacement when your product is genuinely better.
- Case studies for tools you use.
- Reporter/source responses when you have credible expertise or data.
- Product-update outreach to writers who already cover the category.
- Broken-link replacement with a genuinely useful asset.
- Affiliate/referral programs after the product converts.

Avoid:
- Cheap backlink packages.
- Spammy directories.
- Forum/comment spam.
- Private blog networks.
- Over-optimized anchor text.
- Irrelevant link swaps.
- Acquired-domain redirects without topical and backlink-history due diligence.

For `$1k+ MRR`, a backlink plan should list 10 specific target pages/sites and why each would rationally link. For pre-revenue, prefer product/demand proof unless the SEO asset itself is the MVP.

## Metrics And Tools

Use tools only to answer the next MRR question:

- Ahrefs/Semrush/Mangools: demand, competition, content gaps, backlinks.
- Google Search Console: queries, impressions, click-through, indexed pages.
- Analytics/PostHog: landing conversion, activation, paid conversion.
- Stripe/payment data: whether SEO traffic pays.

Ask for at most 1-2 missing metrics. Do not create a dashboard project.

## Output Add-On

When this reference is relevant, add only these fields to the default output:

- `Search-demand signal`: main query/job, demand strength, and confidence.
- `Intent asset`: the page/tool/content type that fits the query.
- `SEO hard gate`: what blocks SEO work, if anything.
- `Next organic action`: one page/tool/outreach action tied to MRR.
